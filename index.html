Bio

Welcome to my personal webpage. I am a data enthusiast with almost two years of hands-on experience in data analysis, both in industry and academia. At present, I work as a Research Assistant at the Innocenzo Gasparini Institute for Economic Research at Bocconi University. Within this role, I actively engage in empirical projects at the intersection of Economics and Data Science. I have a Master's degree in Economics with a major in Big Data and Business Analytics from Bocconi University and a Bachelor's degree in Economics from the University of Pisa. On this webpage, you can find more information about my background, my work, and my hobbies. Have a question or want to discuss a project? Feel free to email me or connect with me on LinkedIn. Thank you for visiting!

Research Experience

During my Master studies I have developed a keen interest in the application of quantitative methods and computational tools to economic research. In April 2022, I started working at the Innocenzo Gasparini Institute for Economic Research at Bocconi University as a Research Assistant, under the supervision of Prof. Francesco Decarolis. During this experience, I have been primarily working on two research projects. The first project covered topics in the field of Artificial Intelligence and algorithmic bidding in sponsored search auctions (link). The second project studies the presence of complementarities in the digital advertising industry by estimating a structural model for many-to-many matching games (link). The main responsibilities encompass data cleaning and manipulation of large and complex datasets, data visualisation, training of supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning algorithms, and the development/optimisation of modules and scripts in R/Python. At the same time, my Master thesis gave me the opportunity to develop an original research project where I studied the effect of recommender systems on consumer behaviour on music streaming platforms.
